# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

This site serves three overlapping audiences: hiring managers evaluating fit, prospective clients deciding whether to trust the work, and peers in the design and development community assessing craft. Visitors arrive to quickly understand the maker's taste, capability, and point of view, then decide whether to reach out, keep exploring, or remember the work.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to establish credibility through the quality of its presentation and the clarity of its work. The redesign makes the owner look bold, distinctive, and unafraid of strong visual choices — someone who leads with taste and backs it up with execution. Success means a visitor leaves with a strong sense of craft, trust, and memorable personality.

## Brand Personality

Bold, precise, and visually confident. The voice is deliberate and dramatic — not loud for its own sake, but unapologetically present. The dark aesthetic signals someone who works with focus and intensity, not someone who follows safe conventions.

## Anti-references

Do not make this look like generic SaaS marketing with metrics blocks, templated gradient heroes, or interchangeable startup polish. Do not default to editorial-serif portfolio tropes, magazine minimalism, or art-school presentation patterns that feel performative instead of personal. Do not make it "light mode with a dark toggle" — commit fully to the dark.

## Design Principles

- Credibility through boldness: the interface should prove attention to detail through daring choices, not safe defaults.
- Dramatic confidence: visual decisions should feel composed but striking — never timid or trend-chasing.
- Show discernment fast: visitors should understand taste and capability immediately, with massive hierarchy and zero filler.
- Every element earns its place: avoid decorative scaffolding, repeated tropes, and generic portfolio grammar.
- Make trust actionable: the site should move naturally from impression to evidence to contact.
- Commit to the dark: the void-black background is the stage, not a setting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline, even on dark backgrounds. Motion should always respect reduced-motion preferences, and critical meaning must never rely on color alone. Contrast, keyboard access, and readable hierarchy should be treated as product requirements rather than polish. All animations must have instant fallbacks for reduced-motion users.
