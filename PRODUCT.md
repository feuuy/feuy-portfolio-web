# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

This site serves three overlapping audiences: hiring managers evaluating fit, prospective clients deciding whether to trust the work, and peers in the design and development community assessing craft. Visitors arrive to quickly understand the maker's taste, capability, and point of view, then decide whether to reach out, keep exploring, or remember the work.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to establish credibility through the quality of its presentation and the clarity of its work. The first version should make the owner look thoughtful, capable, and production-ready rather than merely available. Success means a visitor leaves with a strong sense of craft, trust, and professional seriousness.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, and credible. The voice should feel deliberate rather than loud, confident without startup theatrics, and polished without slipping into generic luxury or editorial affectation.

## Anti-references

Do not make this look like generic SaaS marketing with metrics blocks, templated gradient heroes, or interchangeable startup polish. Do not default to editorial-serif portfolio tropes, magazine minimalism, or art-school presentation patterns that feel performative instead of personal.

## Design Principles

- Credibility through craft: the interface should prove attention to detail through execution, not through claims.
- Calm confidence: visual decisions should feel composed and assured, never frantic or trend-chasing.
- Show discernment fast: visitors should understand taste and capability quickly, with strong hierarchy and little filler.
- Every element earns its place: avoid decorative scaffolding, repeated tropes, and generic portfolio grammar.
- Make trust actionable: the site should move naturally from impression to evidence to contact.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline. Motion should always respect reduced-motion preferences, and critical meaning must never rely on color alone. Contrast, keyboard access, and readable hierarchy should be treated as product requirements rather than polish.
