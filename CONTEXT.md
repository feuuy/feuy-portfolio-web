# FEUY Portfolio

The FEUY Portfolio is a personal portfolio site that establishes the maker's credibility through the quality of its presentation and the clarity of its work. It exists to help visitors assess craft, trust the work, and decide whether to reach out.

## Language

**Portfolio**:
The FEUY Portfolio is the public-facing website that presents the maker's taste, capability, and point of view.
_Avoid_: Site, app, project

**Primary Audience**:
Hiring Managers are the first audience the Portfolio is optimized for, with Prospective Clients second and Peers third.
_Avoid_: Everyone, general audience

**Primary Action**:
The main action the Portfolio should drive is starting a conversation with a qualified visitor after establishing credibility.
_Avoid_: Passive browsing, generic engagement

**Credibility Proof**:
The minimum proof the Portfolio must provide is clear evidence of the maker's taste, technical capability, and professional seriousness through a small number of concrete work examples.
_Avoid_: Full archive, life story, vague claims

**Launch Content Set**:
The first release of the Portfolio consists of one homepage, two to three featured case studies, one concise about section, and one contact path.
_Avoid_: Blog, full archive, services sprawl, resume dump

**Homepage-First Structure**:
The homepage carries the Portfolio's main credibility argument, while case study pages provide deeper supporting proof.
_Avoid_: Thin homepage, case-study-dependent persuasion

**Designer-Developer**:
A Designer-Developer is the Portfolio's primary identity: someone who combines strong design judgment with the ability to ship precise, production-ready digital work.
_Avoid_: Creative technologist, full-stack developer, designer who codes

**Work Type**:
Portfolio work may be either shipped work or speculative work, but speculative work must always be labeled honestly and never presented as shipped client or team work.
_Avoid_: Unlabeled concept work, implied shipped work

**Shipped**:
Shipped work is work that was actually delivered or released in a real client, team, or production context.
_Avoid_: Live-ish, near-production, concept

**Speculative**:
Speculative work is self-initiated or exploratory work that is presented honestly as not having been shipped in a real client or team context.
_Avoid_: Hidden concept work, implied client work

**Published Project**:
A Published Project is a Project whose `_status` is `published` and that has cleared the Portfolio's Publication Rules; only Published Projects are reachable from the public homepage or case-study pages.
_Avoid_: Draft, archived, scheduled, hidden concept work

**Featured Project**:
A Featured Project is a homepage-selected project chosen for its credibility yield rather than its chronology.
_Avoid_: Latest project, equal-weight project

**Case Study**:
A Case Study is a dedicated project page that proves judgment, execution, and outcome framing beyond the homepage summary.
_Avoid_: Gallery, technical dump, project card

**Problem/Challenge Framing**:
Problem/Challenge Framing is the early case-study context that explains what the project was trying to address so the reader can evaluate the decisions that follow.
_Avoid_: Context-free showcase, unexplained work

**Role/Context Statement**:
A Role/Context Statement is the plain-language explanation of what the portfolio owner did on a project and whether the work was solo, collaborative, or team-based.
_Avoid_: Ambiguous ownership, metadata-only role labels

**Execution Evidence**:
Execution Evidence is the case-study section that shows how the work was actually carried through in practice, including visual or build evidence as needed.
_Avoid_: Stack badge list, unsupported claims

**Outcome / Learning**:
Outcome is the closing proof section for shipped work, while Learning is the equivalent closing section for speculative work.
_Avoid_: Fake impact language, implied shipped results

**Closing Section**:
The Closing Section is the case-study's final proof block. For shipped work the Closing Section is the Outcome; for speculative work it is the Learning. Exactly one of the two is required for a Published Project.
_Avoid_: Mixed closing sections, swapped outcome/learning, no closing section

**Publication Rules**:
The Publication Rules are the field-level and ordering-level rules that gate a Project from draft to Published Project: required fields per Work Type, at least one Execution Evidence decision, and a unique Editorial Order among Published Projects.
_Avoid_: Loose drafts, ambiguous work-type labels, duplicate editorial order

**Project Preview**:
A Project Preview is the homepage representation of a Featured Project, consisting at minimum of a title, a one-sentence framing line, a Work Type label, and a link to its Case Study.
_Avoid_: Full case study, generic card, unlabeled tile

**Preview Image**:
The Preview Image is the required visual asset used to represent a project in homepage previews and related summary contexts.
_Avoid_: Incidental screenshot, unlabeled shared media blob

**Supporting Images**:
Supporting Images are optional case-study visual assets that reinforce the project's evidence beyond the Preview Image.
_Avoid_: Media dump, decorative gallery

**Strongest-Proof-First Ordering**:
Strongest-Proof-First Ordering is the editorial rule that Featured Projects and Case Study sequencing follow the projects that yield the most credibility rather than chronology.
_Avoid_: Newest-first ordering, automatic chronology

**Editorial Order**:
Editorial Order is the explicit global sequence applied to published projects so the homepage and case-study navigation follow one consistent ranking.
_Avoid_: Implicit sorting, chronology proxy

**Contact Path**:
The Contact Path is the Portfolio's primary conversion mechanism, which in the first release is a direct email action.
_Avoid_: Generic engagement, multi-step funnel

**Direct Email Action**:
The Direct Email Action is the final contact step where a visitor opens or uses the portfolio owner's email address to start a conversation.
_Avoid_: Lead form funnel, booking funnel

**About Section**:
The About Section is a concise homepage section that provides professional context with a small amount of personal texture.
_Avoid_: Bio essay, resume dump, personal memoir

**Working Principles**:
Working Principles are a very small set of concrete professional principles embedded in the About Section and evidenced by the work.
_Avoid_: Generic process claims, standalone methodology section

**Structured Single-Page Homepage**:
The Structured Single-Page Homepage is the main Portfolio route, containing the primary persuasion flow through sections such as Work, About, and Contact.
_Avoid_: Thin landing page, many shallow top-level pages

**Light Sticky Header**:
The Light Sticky Header is a visually restrained persistent navigation layer that helps visitors move through the Structured Single-Page Homepage.
_Avoid_: Heavy app shell, static absent header

**Restrained Footer**:
The Restrained Footer is the Portfolio's composed closing area, repeating the main navigation and secondary contact information without becoming a sitemap block.
_Avoid_: Heavy footer, abrupt page ending

**Work / About / Contact**:
Work, About, and Contact are the conventional navigation labels for the first release of the Portfolio.
_Avoid_: Clever navigation labels, stylized IA terms

**Individual Voice**:
The Portfolio presents one individual person rather than a studio or collective identity.
_Avoid_: Studio voice, agency framing, collective identity

**Personal-Name Identity**:
The public-facing identity of the Portfolio is the person's own name rather than FEUY as a separate visible brand.
_Avoid_: Repo codename as public brand, dual-brand identity

**Role/Value-First Hero**:
The Role/Value-First Hero leads with the Designer-Developer positioning and value proposition, with the person's name immediately adjacent rather than leading alone.
_Avoid_: Name-only hero, abstract personal intro
